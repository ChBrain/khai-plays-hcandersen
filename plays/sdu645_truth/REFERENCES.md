# Truth: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging Hans Christian Andersen's _Truth_ (SDU-645), his children's
parody-comedy first printed in 1940. The current board is an unverified
placeholder: the work's identity is now confirmed on register evidence,
but its actual text is not yet in the house, so the staged court-allegory
is invention pending the source, not a rendering of it.

## Origin

The register settles what earlier searches could not. _Truth_ is a genuine
Andersen work, register vid 645, number 45 in the drama category, **BFN
1266**, first published **13 January 1940** in Eiler Høeg, _Om H.C.
Andersens Afreageren_ (Ejnar Munksgaard, 31 pp), which carries its first
printing. The register gives its full title as **"Truth. Plastisk
declamatorisk Opera uden Musik"** and the bibliography names it a
_børne-parodikomedie_, a children's parody-comedy, reviewed on publication
as "En Børnekomedie af H.C. Andersen." This supersedes the prior
source-verification verdict, which could not reach the register and read
the title as a probable fabrication; it is not fabricated, and it is not
retired.

What the register does not give is the text. The 1940 print is not yet
available to the house, so the board below cannot yet stage the real work:
its Truth-versus-Lie court allegory is unverified invention, keyed to
neither the confirmed genre (a comic declamatory mock-opera for children)
nor any sourced scene. A faithful restaging waits on the 1940 text.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The content is unverified**: the board stages a placeholder, not the
  sourced work; it claims no fidelity until the 1940 text is obtained and
  the production is restaged from it.
- **The register is external**: this warrant answers for one staged tale;
  the full Andersen catalogue and its SDU-645 numbering live in the SDU
  register, not here.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within
  this play's directory.
