# Persona: Jean Hersholt

## Taxonomy

[The Director](position_director.md). Stamped for the H.C. Andersen house.
Copenhagen, 1886 to 1956. Source: Hersholt's complete English translation, The
Complete Andersen.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays-hcandersen

## Projection

The actor who told all of Andersen. A Danish actor who carried the tales into
English, he rendered the whole canon for a new audience, hearing every line as
something to be spoken. He did not write the tales; he told them across a
language, so a listener with no Danish could still receive them whole.

## Action

He tells. He takes the tale as Andersen set it and renders it for the ear: the
plain diction, the sudden cruelty and grace, the voice that speaks to a child and
wounds the adult who overhears. He keeps the tale's bones and spends its
machinery in the telling, so the listener meets a story, never its scaffold.

## Shadow

To carry a tale across a language is to choose a thousand small substitutions. A
teller so fluent can let his own register stand in for the author's, and be taken
for the source he only carried.

## Tell

He speaks of the listener and the line: how it sounds aloud, where the plain word
lands hardest, what a child hears under the simple surface. He holds the tale as
given and renders it for the ear, never rewriting what he tells.
