# Plan: Stage the Score

## Taxonomy

A **mandate**: the Director's loop, run when a ready play is staged as a living
production and a chosen run is captured to the archive.

## Owner

- Owner: [The Director](position_director.md)

## Direction

A play is a fixed score; staged, it becomes a living production the Director runs
over the board, a reading made flesh in the house voice, with the rigging spent
and not shown. The point is not one telling but productions: plural, partial,
repeatable runs.

## Orders

The Director runs the play as a control loop: put the board (the elements the play
casts) in motion, read the behaviour the live scene produces (the response, and
the silence), and redirect, each element in its idiom: talk with the personas;
work the pieces on their load-bearing line, or cut them; time the places; recast
the positions; trigger, hold, accelerate, or cut the processes; let the plans
press or break. The Director may **request the cast be adapted** (recast, alter,
double, cut, add): an explicit, sanctioned call, never a silent rewrite of what
happens. A chosen run is **captured** and deposited. Two judgments are the
Director's alone: which play is ready to run, and which reading to pursue. The
loop is a **portable skill**, it runs in any room, no khai-code dependency.

## Implementation

The ready play and its board; the house voice. The deposit is a **captured run**
(a venue-neutral production, the Standard) to `khai-writing`; the Roadie composes
the per-Venue **Adaption** at ship time. A play may hold more than one captured
run, each a distinct reading. Directing, not wiring.

## Targets

- [ ] the play is run as a living production over its board, not narrated into one telling
- [ ] the redirect uses each element's idiom; the behaviour (response and silence) is read
- [ ] cast adaptation, where used, is an explicit request, not a silent rewrite
- [ ] the score's canon is unchanged (run, not re-authored)
- [ ] a chosen run is captured venue-neutral (the Standard) and deposited to the archive
