# Plan: Stage the Score

## Taxonomy

A **mandate**: the Director's checklist, run when a ready play is staged into a
performance and deposited to the archive.

## Owner

- Owner: [The Director](position_director.md)

## Direction

A play is a score no reader reads as fiction; staged, it becomes a single told
story an audience can receive at a Venue, in the house voice, with the rigging
spent and not shown.

## Orders

The Director reads a ready play - its plot and the elements it casts - and renders
the performance: the house voice spoken, the mechanics **spent, not shown**, the
front-of-house set (byline, blurb, content-warnings, the licence block). Two
judgments are the Director's alone: which play is ready to stage, and which Venue
space it belongs in. The Director authors the performance; the Roadie carries it,
the Archivist keeps it. The render is a **portable skill** - it runs in any room,
no khai-code dependency.

## Implementation

The ready play and its files; the house voice; the Venue's shape. The deposit is
a **venue-neutral performance** (the Standard) to `khai-writing`; the Roadie
composes the per-Venue **Adaption** at ship time. Authorship, not wiring.

## Targets

- [ ] the performance is a single told story in the house voice
- [ ] the mechanics are spent, not shown (no rigging exposed to the audience)
- [ ] front-of-house is set: byline, blurb, content-warnings, licence block
- [ ] the result is venue-neutral (the Standard), deposited to the archive
- [ ] the score's canon is unchanged (staged, not re-authored)
