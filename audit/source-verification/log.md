# Source verification of the unverifiable thirty-nine

Campaign 4 of the content-quality treatment charter. Verdicts, not edits: each of the 39 plays whose declared source the review could not confirm was checked against the SDU H.C. Andersen Centre register, the Hersholt translation index, kb.dk, and theatre-history sources. Data in [`findings.json`](findings.json).

**Method limits.** The environment's network policy blocks fetching the editions directly, so verification ran on search-result snippets; where content could not be established within budget the verdict is an honest `exists-content-unknown`, never a guess. One methodological trap is recorded for future passes: English translations of _Billedbog uden Billeder_ number the evenings differently (Hersholt vs Howitt), so evening plays must be checked against the Hersholt numbering the register uses.

**Board shapes.** Every verdict carries a board-shape recommendation sized to the actual source, because the review found the boards templated: near-identical 15-file sets regardless of whether the source is a two-page sketch or a three-act singspiel. The recommended boards below range from 3 to 18 files with genuinely different type mixes; the restaging campaigns must build to source scale, never to the template.

**Tally:** 2 verified, 22 divergent, 14 unresolved, 1 not found. Recommendations: 4 keep, 20 restage (join campaign 2's queue), 5 redeclare, 10 to the Choregos.

## Resolution (update)

The network limit that forced the `exists-content-unknown` verdicts was
later worked around: the complete Hersholt text of _Billedbog uden
Billeder_ was sourced evening by evening from the H.C. Andersen Centre's
own pages (uploaded snapshots), and every evening was restaged from its
verified text. This closes six of the ten Choregos items above, the six
evenings, and confirms the numbering trap the campaign warned of, the
boards were built from a translation numbered one ahead of Hersholt.
